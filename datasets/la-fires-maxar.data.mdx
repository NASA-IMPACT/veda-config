  ### About

  MAXAR Technologies’ high-resolution 8-band satellite imagery provides critical multispectral remote sensing data used for disaster response, environmental monitoring, and infrastructure assessment. The dataset includes imagery captured by MAXAR’s WorldView and GeoEye satellites, offering spectral analysis across visible, near-infrared (NIR), and red-edge bands. These capabilities allow for advanced land cover classification, vegetation health monitoring, and damage assessments following disasters.

  The imagery has a spatial resolution ranging from 30 cm to 1.2 meters, making it one of the most detailed commercially available satellite datasets. MAXAR’s revisit capabilities allow for frequent monitoring, providing valuable insights for rapid response efforts.

  </Prose>
</Block>

<Block>
  
  <Prose>

  ### What MAXAR Data Offers

  * Ultra-High-Resolution Imagery: With resolutions between 30 cm and 1.2 meters, MAXAR imagery provides detailed views of the Earth's surface, enabling precise damage assessment and environmental monitoring.
  * Multispectral Analysis: The 8-band spectral capabilities (Blue, Green, Red, NIR1, NIR2, Coastal Blue, Yellow, and Red Edge) support applications such as vegetation health assessment, water body analysis, and change detection.
  * Frequent Revisit Times: MAXAR satellites provide near-daily revisits, supporting rapid disaster response and recovery monitoring.
  * Disaster Response & Infrastructure Analysis: The high-resolution and spectral diversity facilitate assessments of urban areas, infrastructure damage, and land cover changes.

  </Prose>
</Block>
