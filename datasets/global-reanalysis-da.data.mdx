  - <Link to='/data-catalog/global-reanalysis-da-5'>HydroGlobe-5km</Link>: 5km resolution with improved data assimilation for enhanced accuracy. Temporal extent 2003-2024

Realistic estimates of water, energy, and carbon cycle variables are crucial for accurately understanding Earth system processes. HydroGlobe provides these estimates by integrating satellite observations with model simulations using data assimilation within NASAâ€™s Land Information System (LIS) modeling framework.

## Dataset Accuracy
The HydroGlobe-10km dataset has been evaluated against both remotely sensed and in situ observations to assess its accuracy. Detailed reference datasets and evaluation results can be found in the Supporting Information section in this paper. While HydroGlobe provides high-quality hydrological reanalysis data, users are strongly encouraged to verify that the dataset meets the accuracy requirements of their specific applications. The interpretation of results should be done with consideration of potential uncertainties inherent to the satellite observations utilized for data assimilation and the corresponding modeling processes.

## Scientific Details
HydroGlobe integrates various remote-sensing datasets to provide real-world hydrological information. 

Key Aspects of the Dataset:

- The model: Both HydroGlobe-10km and HydroGlobe-5km use the Noah-MP land surface model within NASA LIS.
- The meteorological forcings: 
  - HydroGlobe 5km is driven by meteorological fields from the Modern-Era Retrospective analysis for Research and Applications version 2 (MERRA-2), radiation products from NASAâ€™s Clouds and the Earthâ€™s Radiant Energy System (CERES) project, and precipitation from Integrated Multi-satellitE Retrievals for Global Precipitation Measurement (IMERG) that has been downscaled and bias-corrected based on WorldClim precipitation climatology.
  - HydroGlobe 10km is driven by a combination of NASAâ€™s Modern-Era Retrospective analysis for Research and Applications version 2 (MERRA-2) data and Integrated Multi-satellitE Retrievals for Global Precipitation Measurement (IMERG) rainfall data.
- The assimilated remote-sensing datasets:
  - HydroGlobe-10km assimilated combined soil moisture product from European Space Agency (ESA CCI), leaf area index (LAI) from Moderate Resolution Imaging Spectroradiometer (MODIS), and terrestrial water storage anomalies from Gravity Recovery and Climate Experiment and follow-on satellites (GRACE/GRACE-FO).
  - In addition to the remote-sensing products that HydroGlobe-10km adopted, HydroGlobe-5km includes the assimilation of soil moisture from NASA SMAP for 2023-2024 when ESA CCI product is not available and the assimilation of snow water equivalent derived from MODIS using machine learning-based reconstruction approaches.
