    ## Overview

    The May 2022 derecho, which occurred on May 12, brought devastating winds and severe weather to parts of the northern Great Plains, particularly affecting North Dakota, South Dakota, Minnesota, Iowa and Nebraska. The storm produced wind gusts exceeding 100 mph, causing extensive damage to homes, businesses, and infrastructure. The National Weather Service reported numerous downed trees and power lines, leading to widespread power outages affecting tens of thousands of residents. The derecho was accompanied by a series of tornadoes, with the strongest intensity rated as an EF2 (111-135 mph winds) that significantly damaged its surroundings as it [tracked](https://weather.com/storms/severe/news/2022-05-13-may-derecho-dakota-minnesota-nebraska). Overall, the event resulted in 6 fatalities, over 13 injuries, $1.3 billion in property damage, and 200,000 power disruptions, highlighting the severe weather threat that was present over the [region](https://www.nytimes.com/2022/05/13/us/haboob-dust-storm-upper-plains.html). Recovery efforts began promptly to restore services and assist affected communities.
